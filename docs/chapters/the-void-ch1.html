Chapter One

The ...

Inside a dark room, on an extremely cold day, a young girl rose from her neatly, yet rusty torn rags she called bed. She exhaustedly and blindly three her left hand on the left drawer to find a match stick. The candle turb had previously burned down the rusted metallic candleholder. The candle light wavily and brightly lit the room, revealing everything she owned. Everything.
Seated on the edge of her bed, she slumped her head inside her hands that shakily trembled on her thighs. A minute past, she then pushed herself backwards and fell on the bed again; hands clamped on the back of her head. She blankly stared at the roof and unknowingly found herself counting the holes on the roof of her tiny room. 15 holes in total. One of her hands unclamped and wondered freely on the bed, her legs rhythmically tapping on the dusty flour. She hadn’t eaten the previous night but that didn’t make her weak. Poverty crushed her and her mind was in ruins.
