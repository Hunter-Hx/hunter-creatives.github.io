After Everything

At night, when the tie is hung,
And the T-shirts lie scattered,
Socks on the floor,
The kitchen still warm with dinner.
When the wigs are removed,
And the makeup is washed,
The shelf in disarray,
And the vest is on,
The TV is muted and the assignment is are done,
The kids half-asleep and the doors locked,
That's when 'love' happens-
Or whatever they call it.

Frozen Embrace

Wake up boy!
Dig that grave
And release your friends to the waiting angels,,
Is not bad to be lonely, you see?,
The world will not pamper you boy!
Boy! Take that shovel
Mix that sand and gravel
And get messy in it,
Question your flaws, critique with honesty
And if need be, surrender,
The world is not your mom, boy!
Boy! This is your toll,
Sweat all day long,
Endure hunger if provisions fail,
Boy! Future is for you,
You gotta be tough boy!
Boy! Walk the streets,
Cry and get eliminated,
The World has no use for cry babies,
Boy! Your tears better be viscous!

Unfinished Prayer

I prayed with broken sentences,
faith stitched with doubt.
Heaven listened anyway.

Zoning Out

I sit on public transport,
and sometimes I zone out.
Not because I'm tired,
It's just a fascinating dark place,
that I find hard to leave,
and hard to stay.

Absolution

I was not raised in absolution,
this doesn't guarantee me being good
or bad or something worse.
It's the serendipity,
that aligns from calamity.
