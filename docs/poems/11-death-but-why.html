Death, But Why?

From the collection: Silent Cries And Other Poems

One day,
I will rest six feet underground,
As sand and earth lay atop my frame,
Water will trickle over my remains,
The Sun will shine on my grave,
And heat will fill the air inside.

My body will decay and rot,
Maggots and mites will feast,
As my skeleton lies shattered,
Scattered within my new home.

Friends may forget me,
Relatives may remember,
But in the end, it matters not,
For I will be gone, FOREVER.

Why must we face this fate,
To leave this world and disintegrate?
Is there a purpose or a plan,
To this end we all must understand?

But until that final day,
I'll live my life in every way,
And cherish all the moments spent,
Before my body and soul are rent.
